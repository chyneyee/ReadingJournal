# The Dark Half - Stephen King

## Summary
Thad, who was just an eleven-year-old boy in 1960, was experiencing severe headaches for several months. The neurologist, Hugh Pritchard, has diagnosed that there's a tumour in Thad's brain. When Hugh is performing the surgery, he finds a single blind and malformed human eye on the brain, additionally with a nostril, three fingernails, and two teeth. Thad's twin went unabsorbed and ended up in his prefrontal lobe. In the operation, Hugh has removed the unborn twin brother who was absorbed into Thad.

<br>

### Part 1 - Fool's Stuffing
Thad Beaumont is a novelist who lives in the town of Ludlow, Maine, with his wife, Liz, and his twins. Thad has written one book under his name and received a nomination for a literary award but is not very successful. Under the pen name "George Stark", he writes highly successful crime novels about a psychopathic killer named Alexis Machine. When George's true identity is exposed, Thad and Liz decide to stage a mock burial for George at the local cemetery, which is featured in People magazine. His epitaph reads: "Not a Very Nice Guy."

Homer Gamache's wife has reported that her husband is missing. A witness mentions that she saw Homer stop his pickup truck to help a hitchhiker while Homer was driving near Castle Rock. Later, Sheriff Alan Pangborn discovers Homer's brutally beaten body, leading Alan to believe that someone has ripped off Homer's prosthetic arm and beaten him with it.

Frederick Clawson is found dead, and his genitalia and tongue have been cut off. Dodie Eberhart, Frederick's landlady, finds Frederick's corpse at his apartment and calls the police. Frederick's blood is on the wall with the cryptic message – 'The sparrows are flying'. 

Alan and a couple of state troopers go to Thad's house after they have all the required evidence. Alan is so sure that Thad is the killer, but Thad has no idea about the murders. After Thad pushes for the reasons of their visit, Alan reveals that Homer is dead. Thad provides a solid alibi for the time when Homer is killed and wonders why Alan is so convinced that Thad is the killer. Alan believes Thad's alibi, but the fingerprints obtained from Homer's truck are Thad's. Later on that night, Alan visits Thad alone to ask for his help because Frederick's death is linked to Thad. Thad and Liz tell Alan how Frederick figured out that George Stark is the pseudonym of Thad Beaumont and tried to blackmail Thad. Frederick's knowledge of George's identity led Thad to retire George with a mock burial. 

<br>

### Part 2 - Stark Takes Charge
Thad and Liz tell Alan that George Stark is the alter ego who comes to life, but Alan finds it ridiculous to believe. Thad eventually discovers that he and George share a mental bond and begins to find notes from George written in his own handwriting. The notes tell Thad what activity George has been engaging in.

George calls Thad and tells Thad about his plan of killing everyone who misled Thad into burying him. George knows that the authority is listening to their conversation, and he denies that he's George Stark. Then, George calls Thad the second time and demands Thad write a new book, or else he will start harming Thad's family. Thad also surprisingly finds out that George doesn't know anything about birds or the words he wrote on the walls after the killings.

George gives Thad a week to write a book. Thad insists on not writing it, and George threatens to harm Thad's family if he still refuses to write a book under George Stark. Meanwhile, George's flesh is decaying and will soon vanish if Thad still hasn't started writing anything.

<br>

### Part 3 - The Coming of the Psychopomps
<details>
  <summary>Spoiler</summary>

Alan contacts Dr Hugh Pritchard about Thad's tumour; Dr Hugh operated on Thad when he was eleven years old. Dr Pritchard tells Alan that Thad's tumour was actually Thad's twin. His twin was absorbed in utero and located in an unusual place. In short, the foetus Thad once ate his brother in his mother's womb. This leads to questions about whether George is the unborn twin or the alternate personality of Thad. 

Thad eventually destroys George by using a bird call to summon a flock of sparrows that tear George's body apart. The sparrows lift George back to the afterworld.
</details>

## Personal Thoughts
- I find it difficult to dive into the story compared to Needful Things, which is more straightforward.
- I feel the story is interesting and creative, but it's difficult to follow, although the story has a smaller number of characters compared with King's other stories. The slow pace makes the story seem draggy to read. It's still an interesting read and definitely a memorable story but not as intriguing as reading Needful Things.
