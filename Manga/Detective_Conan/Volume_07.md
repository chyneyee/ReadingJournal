### Case 018 - [Moonlight Sonata Case](https://detectiveconan96.fandom.com/wiki/Volume_7#Piano_Sonata_Case)
#### File 062 - The Invitation To Tsukikage Island / Invitation to Moon Shadow Island
Kogoro has received a mysterious invitation from a man called "Keiji Aso" to a small town on Tsukikage Island. He got a strange letter and phone call from the same person. When he, Ran, and Conan arrive at the town hall to ask where "Keiji Asoh" live, the staff can't find the name. His supervisor appears, and once the staff explains the problem and mentions the name, everyone in the Town Hall is shocked. The supervisor tells them that "Keiji Aso" already died over ten years ago.

Keiji was a renowned pianist from the island who committed suicide by setting the house on fire after shutting himself in his house with the family. According to the people who tried to rescue them, he had murdered his wife and daughter with a knife. Afterwards, the villagers saw him repeatedly playing Beethoven's Moonlight Sonata until the blaze consumed him. 

Ran suggests asking the mayor since the villagers mentioned that the mayor was Keiji's friend. Ran sees a woman nearby. She asks the woman where to find the mayor, but the conversation shifts to the looming mayoral election. The woman explains that there are three candidates for the election: Mr Masato Shimizu, the fishermen's representative; Mr Tatsuji Kuroiwa, the current mayor whose popularity is going down; and Mr Hideo Kawashima, the wealthiest man on the island. The woman later introduces herself as Narumi Isa, a doctor in the village. She mentions that the village has a second-anniversary memorial service for the last mayor, Mr Isamu Kameyama. The three candidates should be at the community centre to attend the memorial ceremony.

Meanwhile, the villagers are protesting in front of the community centre. Reiko Kuroiwa, the mayor's daughter, is angry with the protest and commands Kazuaki Hirata, the mayor's secretary, to do something to shut the villagers up. Reiko's fiancé, Shuichi Murasawa, is telling Reiko that he had asked some people in the village, and it seems the election isn't optimistic for the current mayor. While Hideo is teasing the mayor about how he has the upper hand in the election, Kazuaki informs the mayor that a detective from Tokyo wants to see him. Both the mayor and his daughter are shocked.

While Conan, Ran, and Kogoro are waiting for the mayor in the hallway, Conan looks around and finds a piano in a nearby room. Kogoro realises the room is directly facing the ocean outside, while Ran notices how dirty the piano is and almost touches it before being stopped by Kazuaki. He warns Ran that the piano is cursed because it was played by Keiji Asoh the night he died and another similar death happened to the last mayor, Isamu Kameyama. Two years ago, Kazuaki found Isamu at the piano, dead of heart attack, just after hearing the Moonlight Sonata being played. Since then, the residents have never touched the piano. Despite the warning of the curse, Conan randomly plays the piano and is surprised that the tunes are still in good condition. 

Kazuaki asks them to wait at the hall until the service is over. They bump into Narumi, who has just arrived. Narumi comes to the memorial service to pay respects because Kameyama was the first person she performed an autopsy on when she moved there. During the service, the mayor suspects Hideo is playing a dirty trick by hiring a detective to investigate him. Hideo doesn't say much and leaves to the washroom. 

While Kogoro, Ran, and Conan are waiting for the mayor, Conan becomes suspicious that someone has been secretly tuning the piano, as the notes are in tune despite the piano supposed to have been untouched. Suddenly, the sound of Moonlight Sonata appears in the community centre. Conan quickly runs to the piano room and finds Hideo dead at the piano. 

#### File 063 - The Piano's Curse / The Curse Of the Piano
Conan solves the clue from Kogoro's mysterious letter. It was a warning that once again someone would die on the night of a full moon on the island. Kazuoki believes the piano is cursed, while Kogoro denies it because he finds a tape recorder nearby, and it plays the same music to make it look like the other two deaths. 

Conan finds the traces of seawater leading to the back door of the room. Narumi examines the body and concludes that Hideo's cause of death is drowning. Conan agrees with Narumi's finding, pointing out that Hideo's jacket is floating in the sea outside the room and there are marks on the floor, indicating something was dragged from the door to the piano. There are traces of mud and sand on Hideo's back. The door leading to the water and all the windows in the room have been locked from the inside. There is a possibility that the murderer went back to the service after he left the room. 

The other mayoral candidates start to accuse each other of being the murderer. Conan believes that the murderer is a man because it requires strength to handle someone as large as Hideo. The thing that Conan couldn't understand is why the murderer dares to risk bringing the victim back to the room. Kogoro asks about the history of the piano, and Kazuaki claims that the piano is cursed since Keiji donated it fifteen years ago. Later, Kogoro finds a piece of sheet music on the piano that hadn't been there before. Suddenly, Ken Nishimoto runs away from the room. Kazuaki explains that Ken used to be rich and popular, but since Isamu's death, he has become isolated. Ken is an old friend of Tatsuji, though Tatsuji seems reluctant to confirm that they were.

Ran finally found a police officer after running around asking people, and the phone wasn't working. The police decide to question the witnesses in the morning.

#### File 064 - Left-Behind Music / The Musical Score
When Kogoro realises the strange letter is hinting that the murder is just a beginning, he rushes back to the community centre, as he worries that there will be more murders. Conan agrees that they should go too. Ran tries to play the "Moonlight Sonata" following the music score but finds something strange with the sheet music. They wonder whether there is a message behind the music score and whether the murderer might come back to get it. Suddenly, Narumi appears, bringing food to them after she called the lodge and found out their whereabouts.

While eating, Narumi mentions that the former mayor had a terrifying expression when he died and one of the windows in the room was open. When Narumi points to the window, there is a mysterious figure standing outside. Conan and Kogoro try to chase after the unknown man, but they lose him. 

The next day, Inspector Megure arrives at the crime scene. They spend the whole day questioning the villagers who went to the service. The mayor is very upset with what happened and asked Kazuaki to get rid of the piano within the week. Conan finds it strange that Ken Nishimoto is staying after the questioning has finished. When Ken is leaving, Conan tries to follow him but loses it. Then, "The Moonlight Sonata" is playing at the speaker, and Conan rushes to the broadcast room. He sees Ken in a terrifying state and finds out that the mayor had been stabbed to death. 

Inspector Megure is confident that the murderer cannot escape because the police have sealed all the exits. Conan finds a music score written in blood under the chair the victim was sitting on. 

#### File 065 - The Hellfire Secret
Inspector Megure lists out the suspects – Ken Nishimoto, Narumi Asia, Kazuaki Hirata, Reiko Kuroiwa, Shuichi Murasawa, and Masato Shimizu. Reiko defends herself that the police were questioning her around the time that the murder occurred. Narumi was with Ran and Conan when the murder happened. The suspects narrow down to four males. Inspector Megure asks Ken why he was at the broadcast room. Ken says that the mayor asked him to the broadcast room at 6.30 because he had something to tell him. Inspector Megure suddenly remembers that the mayor requested to schedule the questioning session before noon because he had to meet someone in the evening.

While Reiko accuses Masato of being the murderer, Conan is decoding the music scores left by the murderer. With Ran's help, he is able to crack the code from the score. Each key on the piano is assigned to an alphabet, and the note corresponds to each letter. The score written in blood leaves the message – "The Grudge of the Hellfire". The witnesses believe that Keiji is alive and he was the one who left the message. A Tsukikage Island police officer appears to verify that Keiji was dead. He tells them that the police found remains in the ruins of the fire, and the dental records made positive IDs of the family. The entire house was burnt down except for a handwritten score, which was stored inside a fireproof safe. Kogoro and Inspector Megure request to see the music score, but the music score is kept in the storage room, which requires the police to get the key at the police box.

Conan follows the police officer to look for the key. From the conversation between Conan and the police officer, we learn that Isamu, Hideo, Tatsuji, and Ken were the witnesses of the fire. They were also Keiji's childhood friends and used to take piano lessons together. Ken and Keiji were extremely close. The officer mentions that Shuichi came to the island three years ago and used to attend some technical school. He got engaged to Reiko, but the mayor opposed it. Conan also asks him about Hirata, and the police officer tells him that Hirata often has secret meetings with Hideo. Lastly, Conan asks about Masato, and Hirata tells him that Masato has a strong sense of justice and is popular among fishermen, but he has a short temper. 

The police officer finds the key in his messy office after an hour. Everyone got tired of waiting and left. Conan rushes back to the community centre. Ran and the police officer follow, and Conan asks the police officer to open the storage room. Conan hears a noise from the music room and sees a mysterious figure standing near the piano. The unknown man jumps out through the window, and Conan finds Shuichi lying unconscious on the floor. Then, he hears Ran's scream.

#### File 066 - Blood-Stained Button / The Blood-Stained Button
Conan rushes back to the storage room to look for Ran. When he arrives at the door, he sees Ken's body is hanging by a noose inside the room. A tape recorder is found nearby, indicating Ken died within the last 30 minutes, between 10.30 and 11 pm. Ran finds a musical score on the floor, and Conan decodes the message – "Suicide Note". The suicide note is Ken's confession to the killings to prevent them from exposing the crime that the group of five committed a long time ago. Inspector Megure thinks the case is considered closed, but Conan disagrees. Conan points out the absence of a stool or something similar in the room, and there is no reason for Ken to encode his suicide note.

At the piano room, Narumi is treating Shuichi. Conan finds a tuning hammer, which Reiko says belongs to Shuichi. After everyone leaves the room, Conan checks the bottom of the piano because he noticed the suspect was doing something on it. He discovers a hidden compartment at the bottom of the piano and some powder on the floor.

Meanwhile, the photos of the second murder have been developed. Through the photos, Conan notices something unusual after the body was moved. Hirata wants to go out to buy cigarettes, and Conan notices that his left hand is injured. Conan follows him and later tells Hirata that there is some white powder on his sleeve. Hirata panics and drops a box of foreign coins. 

The island police officer finally found the music score that Keiji left behind 12 years ago. Through the music score, Conan finds out that Keiji had not only a daughter but also a son. The police officer suddenly remembers that Keiji had a son named Seiji who got seriously ill and was hospitalised during his childhood.

Conan rushes back to the broadcasting room and looks at the switch buttons. He figures out why the murderer dragged the body back to the piano room, why the same song played at the scene of each murder, and why the murderer left that mysterious code written in blood at the second murder.

#### File 067 - The Secret Of The Name!! / The Secret Behind a Name!!
<details>
  <summary>Spoiler</summary>

Kogoro had followed Conan up to the broadcast room, which gives Conan the perfect opportunity to dart Kogoro unconscious. Kogoro's voice coming from the broadcast speaker interrupts Inspector Megure's pondering of the case and suspects.

Hirata injured his left hand when he smashed the window to escape after he hit Shuichi. He used the piano room for his drug-dealing business with Hideo. They used the hidden compartment underneath the piano to exchange the money and drugs. Hirata called the piano a curse to ward off the villagers from discovering the hidden compartment. He went to the piano room twice after the first murder to retrieve the drugs. Shuichi discovered Hirata retrieving it, and Hirata hit him and fled. 

When Ran found Ken's body, the storage room's door was locked from the outside. The murderer broke the window to get in, killed Ken, and then left through the window. The murderer prepared a suicide note to make it look like a suicide but made the mistake of not leaving a stool near the victim's feet. The murderer left in a hurry after hearing someone was coming to the community centre.

Conan also points out that the estimated time of death was false for the second murder. The blank tape was used to determine the estimated time of death, which was believed to be around 6.30 pm. However, when Conan fell on top of the code written in blood, the writing was not smudged. Human blood takes around fifteen to thirty minutes to dry at room temperature. The blood on the floor was completely dry. Conan mentions that the murderer started the tape on the blank reverse side, extending the time until the start of the song by over 30 minutes. The murderer hid the reverse button by covering the broadcast room console with the dead body. There was a flashing button near the mayor's neck, but it was off after the body was moved. Since only certain people can get close to the body, Conan deduces that the murderer is Narumi. 

The four men – Hideo, Ken, Tatsuji, and Isamu – were responsible for Keiji's death. They took advantage of Keiji's overseas performances to buy and distribute drugs. They use the hidden compartment underneath the piano. When Keiji told them that he doesn't want to co-operate anymore, four of them trapped him in his house with the family and burnt them to death. All of this was written in the encoded score found in the ruins of the fire. It's a confession letter by Keiji to his son before he died.

Conan reveals Narumi is Keiji's son, Seiji. As Inspector Megure processes the revelation, Narumi has left the room. Everyone runs directly to the community centre, where the last link between father and son remains in the piano room, but it's too late—the entire community centre is ablaze with a gasoline-fuelled fire. Despite the flames, Conan appears in the doorway and offers Keiji's last words, which ask Seiji "to live an honest life". Seiji confesses that he wouldn't have to do so many things if he knew the confession letter existed.

Seji tells the story of how he disguised himself as a woman doctor after graduating from med school and came to the island to investigate. Two years ago, Isamu found out that Seiji is Keiji's son. Isamu got scared and told him everything about the drugs, code, and how they killed his father. Then, he had a heart attack and just died at the piano. Seiji killed Ken because Ken tried to destroy the score.

Conan persuades Seiji to leave, but Seiji feels he is no different than those four. It's far too late for redemption or escape, as Seiji's hands are covered with blood. Seiji picks Conan up and throws him through the window to the safety of the grounds outside. Conan tries to run back in, but Ran restrains him just as music from the piano picks out a message in the code.

When they are boarding the ship to leave the island, Ran asks Conan about the coded message that Seiji was playing. Conan reveals the coded message: "Thank you, little detective."

#### File 068 - Shinichi's Sweetheart!! / Jimmy's Girlfriend!!
The finals for the Sunday Cup will be held that night. Ayumi asks Conan which team he would think will win the cup. Both Genta and Mitsuhiko argue to defend their favourite player, respectively. This leads Conan to ponder about his life and wish that he could go back to high school and play football. He wonders when he can go back to becoming Shinichi, the high school detective.

When Conan arrives at the P.I. office, he sees a girl standing in front of the door. The girl seems hesitant to ring the doorbell, but Conan invites her to come in if she's looking for a private investigator. The girl's name is Ryoko Akagi, a freshman at Beika High School, and she is looking for someone. When she passes the photo of the missing person, Conan, Ran, and Kogoro are shocked to see that the person in the photo is Shinichi. Ran explains to Ryoko that Shinichi has been away from home because he is entangled in a complicated case. Conan asks Ryoko the reason for looking for Shinichi, and she tells him that they are dating. Both Conan and Ran are shocked with the revelation. Ryoko reveals that she even has kissed Shinichi before. Kogoro wonders if Shinichi is living at some girl's place with the excuse of working on a case. Conan tries to defend Shinichi, but Ran is extremely angry and doesn't want to hear any explanations. Conan wonders what Ryoko's motive is for saying such a thing.

Then, a call comes from Dr Agasa reminding Conan the new gadget needs to recharge frequently, and Conan quickly responds that he will tell the rest. Ran asks Conan whether the call was from Shinichi, and he tells Ran that Shinichi asks her not to worry and that he will call Ryoko later. Ran suggests going to Ryoko's house to wait for Shinichi's appearance. Kogoro is not interested in teenagers' romance, so he's not going. Conan agrees with Kogoro, but he has to go after Ran gives him a glare.

Ryoko is wondering if Ran has feelings for Shinichi because she has been angry since she told them about the relationship, but Ran denies it. Conan notices that the door's lock has some scratch marks. When Ryoko wants to make tea, she seems not to know where the tea is. When Ran is asking Ryoko when she and Shinichi started dating, the phone suddenly rings. An unknown man asks her whether someone has already arrived, and she replies that he hasn't come yet. Ran asks Ryoko who was on the phone. Ryoko replies to Ran that her brother was telling her that he'll be working late and they live together. Her brother teaches P.E. at the university. Suddenly a ball falls from the cupboard. Ryoko responds that the ball belongs to the brother, who coaches the football team. Conan feels strange because that ball is for kids and it's a recent model. Conan asks for the bathroom, and Ryoko answers him by guessing that the bathroom is near the entrance door. Conan finds the shoe cabinet only has men's and kids' shoes. He looks around to investigate, and he finds a room that looks like it has been ransacked.

#### File 069 - Detective Ran / The Great Detective Rachel!?
In the room, Conan finds a photo of a boy with a high school boy. He assumes the boy is Mamoru based on the name plate hanging on the room's door. 

Ryoko turns on the TV to watch the Sunday Cup final, which makes Ran think that Ryoko tries to avoid talking to her. Suddenly, Conan sees a familiar face on the TV. The football player appears in the photo with Mamoru, and his name is Hideo Akagi, who plays for the Tokyo Spirits. Conan remembers that a magazine report mentioned that Hideo lost his parents two years ago and lived alone with his little brother. 

Something comes to Conan's mind, and he rushes to the bathroom. He reveals the new gadget invented by Dr Agasa, which is a portable bento box. The portable bento box has a fax machine in it. He makes a call to Ryoko and asks whether Mamoru has been kidnapped. He is guessing that the kidnapper asks Hideo to throw away the game. The kidnapper probably threatened to kill Mamoru if she called a detective or police. For that reason, she claims that she is dating Shinichi so she can have a high school student enter an apartment without raising suspicions. He asks Ryoko to write down the details of the kidnapping and fax the details to him to avoid Ran knowing it. Ryoko is crying while listening on the phone, and Ran is guessing whether Shinichi is on the phone. Ryoko passes the phone to Ran so that Shinichi can talk to her. When Ran shouts on the phone, she can hear her voice, which makes her believe that Shinichi is in the apartment.

Ran is looking for Shinichi around while Ryoko is faxing the details of the kidnapping to him. Mamoru should have been kidnapped between last night and that morning. The kidnapper left a threatening message, demanding Hideo lose the game if he wants his brother back. Mamoru was also forced to write a letter to beg Hideo to save him. 

Hideo accidentally scores a goal. Ryoko immediately receives a fax saying the deal is off and Hideo will not be able to see the younger brother again. 

#### File 070 - A Time Limit on Life!? / Running Out of Time
Conan sees a video game in the room and plays it. Based on the weird letter by Mamoru and the characters created in the video game, he figures out who the kidnapper is. When Conan asks for more information about Hideo's friend, Naoki Uemura, in the toilet, Ran hears Shinichi's voice and almost hit the door using karate. Ryoko tells Ran that Shinichi has something personal to tell her on the phone. When Ran grabs the phone, Conan dashes out from the toilet and pretends that he falls down. He tells Ran that Shinichi just ran out of the door in a hurry to help Mamoru. Ryoko asks whether Shinichi is going to Naoki's place, and Ran drags Ryoko to go to Naoki's house.
