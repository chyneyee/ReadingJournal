# Silence - Shusaku Endo

## Storyline
- News reached the church in Rome. Christovao Ferreira had apostatised after the torture of 'the pit' at Nagasaki. He was sent to Japan by the Society of Jesus in Portugal. Christovao is an experienced missionary who has spent thirty-three years in Japan.
- In 1587, the regent Hideyoshi had started a horrific persecution of Christianity.
- The apostasy of Ferreira has shown the humiliating defeat for the faith itself and for the whole of Europe. 
- Father Rubino and his four companions were finally permitted to sail. Three more priests who had been Ferreira's students were planning to secretly enter Japan. These three men, Francisco Garpe, Juan de Santa Marta and Sebastian Rodrigues, were planning to investigate the matter of Ferreira's apostasy.
- Sebastian Rodrigues documented his account of difficulties they might have experienced travelling to Japan when they were settling in Macao. They met a Japanese man in a town who wanted to return to his country. They decided to bring him along to Japan as a guide, although he is a drunkard and a coward. Only Sebastian and Francisco will travel to Japan because Santa Marta is too ill to do so.
- They landed at one of the villages in Japan and met the Christian villagers. They held masses, baptisms, and confessions, but everything was done discreetly for the safety of the villagers and themselves.
- They were still determined to look for their teacher, Ferreira, to find out the truth about the apostasy.
<br>

## Personal Thoughts
- The dilemma of the priest in between keeping his faith and protecting human lives. They strive to keep the religion alive but feel challenged by the deaths of innocents, which they regard as God's silence.
- I feel the movie is better in expressing the emotions and delivering the intended messages to the targeted audiences. The movie brought out the best of the story, whereas the book seems to be getting duller as it progresses. Towards the end, I feel it's like reading a documentary about the history of Christianity in Japan.
