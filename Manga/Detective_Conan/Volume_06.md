# Detective Conan Volume 06 - The Last Loan

## Summary
### Case 014 - Conan Edogawa Kidnapping Case
#### File 051 - [The Truth Under the Mask](https://www.detectiveconanworld.com/wiki/Volume_6#File_051_-_The_Truth_Under_the_Mask)
Conan is trapped between the two agents from the black organisation: the man in the mask and the woman in black. Fortunately, the door of the hotel room in the hallway opens, blocking the view of the woman in black. A mother leaves her little boy in their room while she goes out, but when the boy closes the door, he finds Conan has sneaked into the room to hide. Conan convinces the boy he's not a thief, then borrows the room phone and a package of gum.

Conan calls a room service for room 301. When it arrives, the woman denies that they made any requests, but the masked man in black orders them to bring it in. He throws the bellhop out and finds gum in the keyhole to avoid automatic locking. Then, he proposes a toast to their extra guest, who is not hiding beneath the cart but inside the room's closet. The man aims a gun at Conan. Conan tries to use his tranquiliser watch but is only informed that the masked man already disabled the watch when they took him earlier.

The masked man fires his gun, but rather than a bullet, a suction dart hits Conan in the forehead. All three adults burst out laughing, and the masked man reveals himself to be Shinichi's father, Yusaku Kudo. The woman in black and the masked contact turned out to be his mother, Yukiko Kudo, and the big man in the mask is Agasa. 

Conan is upset by the set-up, but Yusaku explains that the plot aims to test Shinichi's detective skills. Yusaku is impressed that Conan was able to track them down to the hotel, although he got himself shot in the end. Yusaku is disappointed with Conan for failing to recognise him despite having put a lot of effort into impersonating the Night Baron, a character from his own detective novels. Conan complains he had other things to worry about, especially his life. 

Yusaku and Yukiko offer Conan the chance to leave the country with them so that they don't have to worry about the dangers or risk his life again. Their little show was intended to demonstrate how dangerous Conan's life is if he is chased by the men in black. Yusaku even offers to use his contacts at Interpol to chase the men and get the drug so Conan can return to being Shinichi. Conan refuses to leave and is determined to solve the case by himself, privately not wanting to leave Ran behind in Japan. Yukiko protests, but Yusaku seems to understand (both the determination and knowing Shinichi's feelings for Ran). In the end, "Fumiyo Edogawa" returns to ask the Mouris to continue taking care of Conan. She gives Kogoro a large cheque for expenses and flatters Ran with how much Conan loves staying with her.

On the plane, Yusaku admits that the visit was worth leaving his editors hanging without a finished manuscript. Yukiko worries about Shinichi, but Yusaku assures her that Conan will come begging for help soon. They plan to do more travelling, but a horde of editors suddenly rushes to Yusaku to demand their promised manuscripts, preventing them from going anywhere. Conan alerted the editors as revenge against his parents for their trick.

### Case 015 - [Antique Collector Case](https://www.detectiveconanworld.com/wiki/Volume_6#Antique_Collector_Case)
#### File 052 - The Three Visitors
Denjiro Maru, chairman of the Maru Group, has hired Kogoro to investigate whether his wife has an affair with someone. Kogoro brings Conan and Ran along, but they wait in the garden. While Kogoro is reporting the investigation to Denjiro, a visitor comes. Denjiro goes to the annexe to greet the visitor. Then, the phone rings until it stops. Kogoro waits for Denjiro's return for two hours. The phone rings two more times, and each time, it stops after three rings. 

When Kogoro is on his way to the annexe, the housemaids are shocked to see the strangers in the house. Denjiro's wife, Ineko Maru, appears, and Kogoro accidentally reveals his identity. When the maids go to the annexe to look for Denjiro, they scream after discovering that Denjiro has been stabbed by a sword. 

Based on Inspector Megure's analysis, he concludes that the messy room and numerous lacerations on the body show that Denjiro fought with the killer until he died. Conan says that he saw the visitor's legs and probably left the place through the back gate. The police find an appointment book from the victim's pocket, and there are four names, including Kogoro's, appearing on that day's date. They also find that Dr Hatano was loitering in front of the house. He is Denjiro's physician who comes to the house once a week to perform a routine checkup. The police also find Makoto Akutsu, the sculptor, in the house. Then, Yuji Suwa appears, and Conan checks on Yuji's left hand and asks Yuji whether he is an Iai swordsman. Now, all four people whose names are on the appointment date are at the house.

#### File 053 - Three Men Without Alibis / Alibis of the Three
Yuji confirms that he is an Iai swordsman, and he got the scars on his left hand when he was inexperienced. He came to the house to repay Denjiro the money he had borrowed. Kogoro accuses Yuji as the murderer, and three of them are shocked to hear the news of Denjiro's murder.

Ran notices that the victim is holding the sword the wrong way. Conan mentions that the photo in the room also shows that the victim is holding the sword with the right hand in front, which is completely opposite to how it is positioned now. This indicates the perpetrator deliberately placed the sword in the victim's hand. The room is full of sword marks to make it appear as if the victim had a sword fight and to frame Yuji. The killer made a mistake in putting the sword in the victim's hand, which indicates that the killer knew Yuji would be coming on that day.

The three men explain their reasons for the visit, but none of them have an alibi to prove their statements. Conan smells a woman's perfume from Dr Hatano and finds out that it belongs to Ineko, Denjiro's wife. Dr Hatano tells Megure that he was watching a movie between 3 and 5 pm. When Megure asks him for the movie's name, he can't answer it. Conan exposes Dr Hatano's life by mentioning a movie which ended the week before. Ineko has to tell the truth of their affair to protect Dr Hatano, but they don't have anyone to prove what they said. 

Megure asks all four of them for their appointment time. Aside from Kogoro, the others tell Megure that they left a message on the answering machine about their changes for the arrival time. When they check the answering machine, Makoto's message isn't there. He insists that he is not lying and reveals that Denjiro threatened him to sell Makoto's teacher's masterpiece if Makoto couldn't pay back the money that he borrowed. They find his teacher's sculpture in the annexe, but Conan feels something strange about it.

#### File 054 - Answering Machine Mystery / The Mystery of the Answering Machine
Denjiro seized Makoto's teacher's masterpiece, a dragon sculpture, from Makoto as collateral for the debt. Conan points out that the dragon sculpture remains undamaged in the room after the fight. This leads Kogoro to accuse Makoto of being the murderer, but Makoto insists on the existence of his voice message. Conan remembers the phone's ringing. The first time when the phone rang, it rang for a long time, and Makato claims that he left a message after the phone rang for quite some time. Conan found something strange with the three men's appointment time. He suspects one of them is lying about the appointment time. Although all three men had different appointment times, they arrived simultaneously after the body was found and around the time when the police arrived.

Conan notices the scratch marks on the tansu chest don't look right. He takes a photo of the tansu chest and uses a cutter to rearrange the drawers in the photo. Once he finds the answer, he uses the tranquiliser watch on Kogoro to reveal the truth.


#### File 055 - The Testimony of the Tansu Chest / Words on the Chest

The killer is Yuji Suwa. He purposely put the sword on the wrong hand to avoid being a suspect. Yuji also purposely left the dragon sculpture undamaged to shift the suspicion to Makoto. 

Yuji denies his act and claims that he never met Makoto before. Conan believes Yuji probably heard Makoto's message, including his threat to kill Denjiro if Denjiro sells the sculpture. He rewound the answering machine's tape and erased Makoto's message. Conan wonders why Yuji wants to arrive earlier than the appointment date if Denjiro is not at home. Yuji claims that he knew Denjiro was at home because Denjiro told Yuji that he would be meeting other people when they arranged the schedule to meet.

Lastly, Conan points out the disconnected scratches on the tansu chest. The murderer intentionally rearranged the drawers to remove the clue. Conan asks Megure to rearrange the drawers into the correct position, and the scratches show Yuji's name. Conan suspects that the victim possibly scratched the killer's name on the chest with the sword as he was dying when Yuji was distracted by Makoto's phone call. When Yuji saw it, he rearranged the drawers and left more scratches over it to make the letters unreadable. He put more sword marks all over the room to prevent anyone from noticing it.

Yuji reveals that he also left his family sword as collateral for his debt. Denjiro told Yuji that he already sold the sword and the money from the sale would be the interest from Yuji's debt. Without hesitation, Yuji grabbed the sword displayed in the alcove and swung at Denjiro. Denjiro didn't die on the first swing and wrote Yuji's name when he turned his back.

#### File 056 - The Junior Detective League! / Formation! The Detective Boys
The Junior Detective League is formed and consists of Ayumi, Mitsuhiko, Genta, and Conan. Their first case is to find a missing cat called Akira for their classmate, Hiroki. When they are looking for the missing cat, they hear a cat's sound from a random house. The cat appears to be Akira, and it runs towards them in a bloody body. Conan finds the blood doesn't belong to the cat, and he rushes to check on the house. The rest follow him, standing on the garbage bin to take a peak through the window. They find a dead man collapsed on the floor with his head buries into a bathtub.

When the police arrive, the body disappears.

#### File 057 - Mysterious Brothers 
The kids are definite that they saw the dead body. Megure directs the police officers to search for the dead body while the house owner decides to take a nap upstairs. The younger brother comes home, and Megure tells him what happened. His name is Tomofumi Tanaka, and he works for a brokerage firm. His elder brother, Kazuyoshi, is a writer. Conan finds a leaf flying out from Tanaka's pants. 

The police couldn't find the dead body in the house. Conan suspects that someone probably tied the body together and used the tree to get over the fence. When Tomofumi hears what Conan said to Megure, he pretends there is a call and he answers the phone. The police officer tries to conduct an experiment based on Conan's analysis. They figure that there's an embankment on the other side of the fence. There is a ledge about 40 cm wide, which would be impossible to drag a body on and barely hold a single person. 

Megure and his team leave the house angrily and believe that the kids are lying to him. The Junior Detective League comes to the house at night to investigate.

#### File 058 - Movie Corpse Mystery / The Mystery of the Moving Body
Conan sneaks into the bathroom to investigate the mystery of the disappearing body. The rest are waiting at the phone booth, and they will call the police once Conan contacts them. The Junior League Detectives badges have a miniature transceiver built into them. They can receive signals by adjusting the dial to the right frequency and pushing the button to transmit. 

Conan goes to Kazuyoshi's room to investigate. Tomofumi hears something and comes to the room to check. When Tomofumi is leaving, coincidentally, Genta is shouting via the transmitter. Tomofumi hears the voice and looks for the culprit.

Conan reveals that the dead body is Kazuyoshi. Kazuyoshi has been sitting in the same position since the afternoon, pretending that he is watching the TV and later falling asleep in the chair. When the kids hear Conan's revelation, they quickly make a call to the police. 

Before the police arrived in the afternoon, Tomofumi washed the blood off from his elder brother and dressed him in a bathrobe and sat him down on a chair in the room. He later disguised himself as his elder brother when Megure arrived. While the police were looking for the body, he pretended that he was upset and went upstairs. He went out through the bedroom's window and used the tree to get over the fence. Once outside, he removed his disguise and came back through the front door as himself. 

Tomofumi staged the scene of the elder brother turning off the TV when the police were in the room by using the VCR timer to turn it off. He recorded the angry shout on the answering machine before the police arrived and used the speed dial from his cell phone to look like the brother was yelling from upstairs. He saw the call light flashing on the phone, and he picked it up even though the phone never rang to prevent the answering machine broadcasting the recorded shout. 

When Genta is explaining the details to Megure on the phone, Megure doesn't believe him. Ayumi places the phone near the badge when Megure is about to hang up. Tomofumi confesses that he killed his brother and he is about to hit Conan with a golf club. Tomofumi killed Kazuyoshi because his brother found out about the illegal stock trading and promised that he wouldn't tell anyone as long as Tomofumi continued supporting his life. Kazuyoshi also refuses to leave the house, although Tomofumi is getting married soon. Tomofumi killed Kazuyoshi with the golf club. 

Later, the police arrive. The Junior Detective League's credibility is restored. Their first case, which began as a search for a missing cat, is successfully closed.

### Case 017 - [Tenkaichi Festival Case](https://detectiveconan96.fandom.com/wiki/Volume_6#Tenkaichi_Festival_Case)

#### File 059 - Night of the Festival / Festival Night
Conan, Ran, and Kogoro are celebrating the Tenkaichi Festival at Saitama. When the three characters light up on the mountains, a stranger asks Ran whether she can take a photo of him with one of the mountains as a background. The stranger explains that the photos are for his research because he is working on a travelogue about regional festivals. He gives Ran a book written by him, Norikazu Sasai. He even mentions that he used to write novels under the pseudonym "Tomokazu Imai". The recent winner of the Naomoto Award, Satoru Imatake, used the same pen name when he made his debut. Sasai claims that they used to write together, and they came to that area on that day to celebrate Imatake's win.

Then, Sango Yokomizo, a police officer from the Saitama police, arrives. He has a few questions for Sasai about Imatake because Imatake was murdered in the hotel room where they stay. 

Imatake is a novelist, and he is murdered by a gun. The guests in nearby rooms heard the gunshot. The crime occurred a few minutes after 8 pm. The suspect is a young man who fled from the room right after that. There is some toothpaste in the victim's mouth. Kogoro examines the victim and finds out that the victim's money from the wallet is missing and the room has been ransacked. He suspects it was a burglary and accused Sasai of being the murderer. Sasai denies the accusation and tells them that he was at the Tenkaichi Festival. He claims that the film in his camera can prove his alibi. Koguro requests that the film be developed immediately.

Conan is looking for something in the bathroom until he sees the forensic technician is holding a plastic bag with a toothbrush in it. The forensic technician tells Conan that they discovered the toothbrush near the body and the room was messier when they arrived at the scene. Conan deduces that the victim knew the murderer. 

After an hour, the police have the photos, which proves that Sasai was celebrating the festival. The photo also shows the "Ten" character illuminated on the mountain. Each character burns out about two to three minutes after the following one is lit. The characters are lit in twenty-minute intervals on three mountains. The "Ichi" was lit around after 8.40 pm. The crime occurred at 8.02 pm. The "Ten" should disappear 25 minutes after 8 pm. It's impossible to get to the festival from the hotel in 25 minutes. The photo proves that Sasai was at the festival before 8.25 pm.

#### File 060 - A Perfect Alibi? / The Perfect Alibi?
Detective Yokomizo still believes that Sasai is the murderer, but Koguro feels that Sasai has a perfect alibi. Conan wonders why Sasai is not checking whether he has lost any valuables in the room, and Sasai defends himself, saying that he forgot to do so because his friend just died.

Then, Imatake's editor, Yamada, rushes to the hotel after he receives the news from the police. He mentions that Imatake has a deadline the next day, and he feels that it's too late to ask for another writer. Sasai immediately finds his manuscript and passes it to the editor. The editor accepts it and offers Sasai to write for Literary Art monthly. Before Sasai leaves, he taunts Detective Yokomizo and mentions that he plans to hurry home to start working for the sake of poor Imatake, who was killed by some idiot thief. 

Conan asks Sasai about Imatake's personality, and Sasai says that Imatake was a neat person who always shaved before leaving the house. Conan wants to report it to the police, and Sasai wonders what Conan finds out. Conan points out that the toothpaste around Imatake's mouth indicates he must have been brushing his teeth before being killed. The new finding is different from the initial belief that he was shot when he opened the door. People don't usually greet strangers at the door with a toothbrush in hand and toothpaste in their mouth. The new theory indicates that the victim knew the killer. 

Conan also remembers that when they met Sasai at 8.40 pm, the first words that came from Sasai were 'I made it.' It is strange for someone to say something like that if he arrived at the festival from the beginning. Conan looks at the photo again, and he figures out the trick.
