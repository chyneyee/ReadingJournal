# Grotesque - Natsuo Kirino

## Summary
* Two prostitutes are murdered in Tokyo. Twenty years ago, they were educated at the same elite school for young ladies.
* This story divides into several parts, which consist of the following: - The narrator's account of her life, the narrator's sister's (Yuriko) diary, the murderer's account of his background and the murders, and the narrator's classmate's (Kazue) diary. The three narrators' accounts also reveal some of the unexpected aspects of their other classmates' lives as well as a letter of a teacher's reflection on his mistake in the past.
* The narrator begins her story with her family background, describing her father as Caucasian and her mother as Japanese. The narrator has a younger sister called Yuriko. Yuriko was exceptionally beautiful, but she was murdered two years ago. Yuriko was a prostitute, and she was found dead in her apartment in Tokyo.
* The narrator's former classmate Kazue Sato was murdered less than a year after Yuriko died. Both died the same way and were allegedly killed by the same murderer. The narrator is more shocked by Kazue's death than Yuriko's. Kazue has a normal job during the day but works as a prostitute at night. 
* The narrator tells the story of her childhood life, which consists of her jealousy, anger, and inferiority caused by Yuriko's beauty. She has a poor relationship with her family, which stems from her detestation for Yuriko. 
* The narrator recalls the news of Kazue's death and her conversation with Kazue after Yuriko's death. It leads the narrator to describe more about her life in an elite high school, where she met Kazue. When something unexpected happened in the family, Yuriko returned to Japan from Switzerland and was admitted to the elite school.
* Yuriko's death leads the former guardian to pass Yuriko's diary to the narrator. The narrator claims the diary is filled with lies about her and her mother and doubts Yuriko's handwriting and ability to write a diary.
* Yuriko's diary offers us the details of her charm on older men and how she becomes a prostitute. She considered herself a nymphomaniac. 
* Kazuo's diary fills with her rage against everyone, especially men and her mother. The diary's main focus is her life working in a prestigious firm during the day and being a prostitute at night. Surprisingly, throughout the diary, we can deduce that there are a few similarities between her and the narrator, especially their hatred of men.

## Personal Thoughts
* As the story progresses, I realised the narrator is more problematic than everyone she claims to be. The narrator is a self-centred person who despises everyone's weaknesses. She feels her sister is the culprit of every misfortune that happens to her.
* It's interesting to read the various unusual families and the differences in students' family backgrounds, especially the non-wealthy families' struggles to survive in the elite school.
* When bullying happens, the bully usually moves on with their life, but the bullying victim has been unwillingly pushed to the edge or to a different world with the unforgettable torturing memories.
* One is overproud of her intelligence but feels inferior in her appearance. The other is taking pride in her beauty, but at the same time, her beauty brings disaster to her life, as everyone treats her as an object more than a human.
* Superiority towards those who have lesser quality than them, such as beauty and intelligence. Jealousy of those who are better than them or something that they couldn't achieve. They only can see other people's weaknesses but not themselves.
* Why does everyone feel that others are jealous of them?
* It's interesting to read a story that offers a different perspective on prostitution-related murders, which allows us to delve into the various backgrounds of the victims and steer us away from an instant judgement on the victims' occupation.
