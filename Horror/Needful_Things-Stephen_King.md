# Needful Things - Stephen King

## Summary
### Part 1: Grand Opening Celebration
In a small town, Castle Rock, there is a new shop called Needful Things. Brian Rusk, an eleven-year-old boy, is interested in anything new. The shop is open one day earlier than the announced grand opening. After thinking of impressing his mother with the latest news about the shop, Brian decides to enter the new shop, and without his knowledge, he becomes the first customer who meets Leland Gaunt, the proprietor of Needful Things, before anyone else in Castle Rock.

Brian is intrigued by Leland's friendliness and the uniqueness of the items sold in the shop. Leland asks Brian what he wants the most at that moment, and Brian blurts out that he wishes to have a specific baseball card. Leland goes through his inventory stock and finds the requested baseball card which has the player's signature and a written message to Brian. Leland tells him that the card has two prices – half is cash and the other half is a deed. When Brian comes out of the shop, he can't recall they made a deal, but he finds the autographed card from his packet and only remembers that Wilma Jerzyck's name had been mentioned.

All the items, whether trash or treasure, in the shop have no price tags on them. The residents are excited about the items selling in the new shop. Every customer who visits Needful Things pays a low price for the desired item and needs to play a prank on someone in the town, based on Leland's instructions. Leland sells average stuff in the shop, but he casts a spell on the customers, leading the customers to believe that they are buying something that they most desired and even becoming obsessed with it to the extent of protecting it at whatever cost. Leland manipulates their obsessions, and the customers follow his instructions on playing pranks on others before they lose their most precious possession. The pranks plant the seed of rage in the town residents, leading them to seek revenge on their enemies or rivals without their knowledge of the pranksters.

<br>

### Part 2: The Sale of the Century
Leland is going to hire an assistant to help him execute his secret plan. He knows Danforth is planning to kill some of the people that he despises before he commits suicide. Leland calls him to prevent him from doing so. He even incites Danforth to go against Alan so that when the time comes, he will reveal his plan and use Danforth to get rid of Alan.

The second part focuses on Leland managing other customers who never visit the shop and those who haven't played the prank as they promised after they visited the shop. He uses his special ability to call the remaining residents to come to the shop and puts pressure on those customers to fulfil their promises to play a prank on the designated person. There's something brewing up, and it's going to be a big chaos in the town.

## Personal Thoughts
- Leland manipulates people's desires or greeds to his advantage. He can read his customers' minds and offers them a low price for the item in exchange for playing a prank on someone. The prankster has no connections with the target, but they have to follow Leland's order to protect their precious. However, the prank leads the victims to believe the trick comes from someone with whom they have grudges. 

- The buildup of the story is very intriguing, but the ending is slightly confusing to me. The confrontation between Leland and Alan is a bewildering one, but Leland's ending is both surprising and interesting.
