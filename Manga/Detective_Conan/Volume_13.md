### Case 034 - Triplet Murder Case
#### File 122 - Who was it They Witnessed? / The Witnesses Are...!?
Conan and Ran are enjoying themselves at the beach house with a private beach that belongs to Sonoko's family. Sonoko's sister, Ayako, is going to get married to Yuzo Tomizawa, third son of Tetsuharu Tomizawa, the head of the Tomizawa financial group. Ayako asks the others to come along because Yuzo wants to introduce her to his family members. Yuzo tries to call his father, but it goes to the voicemail. Sonoko plans to give a proper greeting to Tetsuharu after she knows that he will be coming soon. When she and the rest go inside the house, they are surprised to see Tetsuharu with Ayako. Initially, Yuzo plans to have dinner together to introduce Ayako to his father, but Tetsuharu has cancelled the restaurant reservation and decides to try Ayako's cooking at the beach house. 

Tetsuharu watches his favourite baseball game at Ayako's house. The news of the typhoon interrupts the game's progress, which frustrates Tetsuharu. Ayako tries to cheer him up and offers him coffee, leading Tetsuharu to compliment Ayako's kindness and begin complaining about his other two sons, Taichi and Tatsuji. Taiji doesn't want to take over his business and becomes a writer instead. Although Tatsuji joined the family business, he got engaged to someone without informing Tetsuharu. Yuzo asks his father to stop criticising his brothers and claims he loves Ayako not because she's from the Suzuki family. Tetsuharu gets angry and begins to criticise Yuzo's obsession with painting. He hopes Yuzo will stop painting to join the family business. Yuzo tries to control his anger and decides to leave for his studio, which was built by his father after Yuzo won a prize.

Yuzo leaves the house for his studio in the rain, and Tetsuharu continues watching the baseball game. The game finishes late at night, and Tetsuharu decides to return to his house, which is next to the Suzuki's, and promises to visit them with his sons the next day. Sonoko is unhappy and wonders whether Tetsuharu is planning to come to her house to watch another game the next day, and Ran wonders what Tetsuharu's other sons look like. Sonoko replies that the other sons never attend to her family's parties. Ayako suggests going to bed, but a blackout occurs. From the window, Sonoko sees the Tomizawas' beach house is experiencing a blackout too. Suddenly, they hear a strange sound and an odd cry.  They see an unknown figure outside, appearing in an attempt to kill a person who is lying on the ground with a rock. When the unknown figure turns his head, Ayako is shocked to see it is Yuzo. Conan rushes out and Ran runs after him. Yuzo disappears, and Ayako finds out that the victim who is lying on the ground is Tetsuharu. 

When the detective is questioning the witnesses, Yuzo suddenly appears, and he has no idea about his father's death. Conan accuses Yuzo as the murderer as everyone witnesses the killing, and Yuzo denies the accusation. When Yuzo's brothers, Tatsuji and Taichi, appear, everyone is shocked by the resemblance and realises they are triplets.

#### File 123 - The Triplet Suspects / Triplets Under Suspicion
The police discover that blood splattered on the victim's left arm and his watch is missing.

Tatsuji claims that he was in Okinawa for a business trip, and he just landed three hours ago. Taichi mentions that he left a message on the voicemail about the baseball match and claims that he was working on his manuscript at his apartment in Osaka. Tatsuji and Taichi suspect Yuzo as the culprit after they provide their alibis. Yuzo suddenly remembers that he has an illustrator friend who called him around 11.30 pm to wake him up, but Tatsuji says that Yuzo can tell his friend to go along with his story.

Then, a police officer appears and reports to the detective about the passenger list of the flight mentioned by Tatsuji. The flight arrived thirty minutes ago due to the typhoon. Tatsuji admits that someone took his seat on that day's flight, and he was at Hiromi's in Tokyo last night. Hiromi is Tatsuji's fiancee, and he went to see Hiromi, who collapsed two days ago. He asked someone to replace him in the flight to hide his itinerary from his father, who disapproves of his engagement with Hiromi. Tatsuji claims he was watching the baseball match with Hiromi, and the result only appeared on the news after midnight. Since Tatsuji and Taichi knew the result during the match, the only suspect is Yuzo. Conan tests Taichi with the details of the batter from the baseball match, which proves Taichi gave a wrong statement earlier. Taichi confesses that he listened to the game on the radio at the pachinko parlour and used the message he left on voicemail as his alibi. 

While Conan is analysing the brothers' statements about their alibis, a police officer realises the VCR is blinking because of the blackout the night before. Conan suddenly finds out the suspect has told two lies, and he knows who the murderer is.

#### File 124 - Really Sad Brotherly Bonds / A Lack of Brotherly Love

<details>
  <summary>Spoiler</summary>
  
Conan plans to aim his tranquilliser dart at Sonoko to use her as a mouthpiece, but Ran catches him doing that and stops him. Then, Conan excuses himself to go to the bathroom and try for a second time, but Ran stops him again.  Conan has no choice and decided to include Ran and Sonoko in the investigation. Conan points out that Yuzo mentioned a phone call from his friend around 11:30 pm, but the phone shouldn't be working during the blackout. Sonoko questions whether Yuzo is the suspect, and while she's discussing it with Ran, Conan shoots his tranquilliser dart at Sonoko.

Conan accuses Taichi of lying because pachinko parlours aren't allowed to operate after 11 pm.  Taichi says that he probably listened to the radio in the taxi and reminds them about the message he left on the answering machine. Conan explains that Taichi was listening to the baseball game in the Tomizawas' house and left the message by adjusting the time on the answering machine. Then, he waited for his father to return and bashed him with a rock at the front gate so that the witnesses could determine the time the crime took place. Taichi says that it's impossible that he knew when his father would return to the house. Conan says that Taichi had put a bug in the victim's watch, which gave him the details of the victim and the baseball game, and later removed the watch after the murder. Taichi denies that he adjusted the time on the answering machine and says the police have checked that the time is correct. Conan points out the power outage the night before, which caused the answering machine to be unable to record messages. Taichi questions Yuzo's alibi, who mentioned that he got a phone call at 11:30 pm, but Conan reminds him that phone service still works during a blackout. 

The other two brothers refuse to believe that Taichi is the killer. Taichi tells Yuzo that he is not the good brother that Yuzo believes he is and admits he killed his father for the inheritance money to survive as a writer. He went to see his father for help but found out that his father had been pressuring the publishers to turn down his projects so Taichi can succeed him in the family business. He thought about putting the blame on Yuzo so he could get more inheritance and continue with his writing work. Taichi compares his ending to his last work, which ended like a bad mystery. He never expects there will be a blackout and says that maybe he never had the gift of being a writer.

### Case 035 - Illustrator Murder Case
#### File 125 - The Falling Body / The Falling Corpse
Kogoro is invited by the Hanaoka Design Studio to have a discussion with an artist who will soon release a book with a collection of paintings of murder scenes. The discussion will be included in the book for next month's release. 

Meanwhile, the artist, Kenjin Hanaoka, is still sleeping at an apartment but is awakened by his mistress, Izumi Chono, an illustrator who is painting his face with the nail polish. Izumi reminds Kenjin that he's late for the meeting, and Kenjin is not happy with the painting on his face because he doesn't want his wife to see it. Izumi says that she purposely does the painting so that it will lead to a divorce and they can get together in public. Kenjin slaps Izumi and tells her that the relationship is strictly business. Izumi threatens to tell the public that she painted most of the paintings in Kenji's current collection. Kenjin feels that the public won't believe a newcomer like Izumi and claims that he painted more than half of the works. Izumi reveals that she hideously marked all the works she painted under Kenjin's signature, and anyone who knows her signature will recognise it.  She tells Kenjin that he won't be able to get rid of her while putting on her lipstick. At the same time, Kenjin is standing behind her and holding an ashtray, preparing to smash it over her head.

Kogoro has been waiting for more than half an hour for Kenjin. The staff in the studio tries to call Kenjin but to no avail and suggests that Kenjin is probably at Izumi's apartment since she has an illustration due on that day.

Back at Izumi's apartment, Kenjin has killed Izumi and is trying to wash away the blood. Then, suddenly the staff from the studio leaves a message on the voicemail and figures out Kenjin is not there, leading Kenjin to sneer while listening to the message.

In the design office, Conan sees one of the apartment units has left a futon hanging on the balcony despite it raining heavily outside. The staff says that unit is Izumi's, and probably she's sleeping. After waiting for more than an hour, Kogoro decides to leave, and Kenjin appears just in time. Kenjin tells the studio staff that he fell asleep at his studio and also had to arrange someone to pick up Izumi's illustration to deliver it to the editors. Ran asks Kenjin what happened to his fingernails, and Kenjin only realises that his nails are painted with nail polish. Kenjin tells the studio staff to let him talk to Izumi if she calls before he goes to the restroom to wash it off.

When Kenjin comes back from the restroom, Izumi calls into the office. Kenjin talks to her and finds out that Izumi wants to commit suicide. From the office, Kenjin and the others see Izumi jump off the balcony. When Inspector Megure and the police arrive at the crime scene, Kogoro is convinced that it's a suicide. Conan examines the body and finds Izumi is wearing contact lenses and glasses. He deduces that it was a murder and believes that Izumi was already dead before she fell. Conan thinks someone pushed her over and suspects Kenjin is the murderer. However, he hasn't figured out how Kenjin could have pushed Izumi off the balcony while he was at the design office.

#### File 126 - A Suspicious Suicide
Kogoro, Ran, and Conan join Megure and the investigation team to go to Izumi's apartment. When they open the door, they find a nail on the floor. The investigation officer points to Megure about the findings of slippers, a cell phone, and a broken flowerpot on the balcony. Conan finds a trail of shards leading to the drain.  The police search the apartment to look for a will. They find nail polish spilt on the floor, and Conan sees a stain of nail polish on the bedsheet. Conan remembers that Izumi isn't wearing any nail polish, and the last time he saw that nail polish colour was on Kenjin's nails. Kenjin claims that the paint on his nail earlier on was an art paint and not nail polish. Conan also finds that there are cleaning solutions in the fridge. He expects Izumi to have a saline solution since she wears soft lenses.

Then, a neighbour says she saw a suspicious man come to the apartment around 6:30 pm. A bike messenger suddenly appears, and the neighbour recognises him as the one who came to the apartment. The bike messenger tells them that he comes back to leave a pickup receipt. He tells Megure that he didn't see Izumi and was told to pick up the parcel just inside the front door at 6.30 pm. 

After hearing the details from the bike messenger, Conan rushes to the drain at the balcony and finds some long strings caught on a fragment of the pot.

#### File 127 - Flowers and Butterfly
<details>
<summary>Spoiler</summary>


Conan puts a button-shaped speaker under Kogoro's collar after Kogoro is hit by the tranquiliser dart. Conan reveals that Kenjin is the killer. He asks Megure to prepare several items to demonstrate the trick used by the killer.

A long fishing line is used to tie the futon, which acts as the victim. The fishing line is pulled over the balcony railing, and the ending loop is used to hook it over the potted plant. Then, the line is pulled so that the pot hangs up against the railing. The other end of the line is hooked on the intercom. The police slide the futon along the line until it's hanging outside the balcony and place another futon on top of it to hide the victim. Then, the loop of the intercom is removed and placed on the nail. The line is loosened until the nail reaches the door. When someone opens the door, the line is free, and the body would automatically fall from the balcony. The remaining line later ties it between the potted plant and the victim and passes it through the inside and outside of the drain lid. The line ties around the soy sauce dispenser, and the soy sauce dispenser, which acts as a weight, is placed into the drain under the lid. Once the body has fallen, the line will vanish into the drain. Kenjin hires the bike messenger to pick up the parcel, and it triggers the setup when he opens the door. Kenjin used the voice message tape to act as Izumi's voice and made the call from the bathroom. He pretends as if he talked to her, trying to keep her from committing suicide so that everyone can witness her falling from the balcony.

Then, a police officer comes with a cup of coffee as requested by Conan on behalf of Kogoro earlier. Conan takes the coffee and purposely spills on Kenjin's legs. When Kenjin takes off his socks, it exposes his toenail, which is painted with Izumi's signature butterfly mark. Conan reveals Izumi's signature butterfly mark on Kenjin's paintings and deduces that they argued about the artworks. Kenjin confesses the crime and reveals that it's more than the paintings. He tells them that Izumi was like a butterfly flitting playfully around the flower but eventually monopolised the flower, and the flower started to wither away. Kenjin is afraid of Izumi's talent and says that he had to pluck off her wings to keep her from flying anymore.

### Case 036 - Gomera Murder Case 
#### File 128 - The Vanishing Criminal / The Runaway
Agasa's friend, Daisuke Mikami, has been a director for the Gomera movies for the past ten years. The Detective Boys visit Gomera's filming location at Taiho Pictures. Genta is checking the miniature buildings, and the art director, Ryota Adachi, is angry at him touching the props. Shugo Matsui, who acts as the Gomera, offers to bring the kids to look around. Daisuke tells Agasa that he appreciates the cast who have been working in the movies the past ten years. Suddenly, Osamu Kamei, the film producer, interjects, criticising that monster films are outdated, the high maintenance cost of Gomera's outfit and the ageing cast. Before he leaves for lunch, he informs them that the unedited film is ready at the screening room and asks them to have a look at it. He invites Tomomi Sakaguchi, who plays as the fairy in the movie, to join him for lunch, but she rejects him. It appears that the Gomera movie will be the last. Osamu already offered Tomomi and Shugo a new role, which left Ryota unemployed after the final movie.

Shugo brings the kids to the storage room, and they are excited to see all the props from the Gomera movies. Shugo goes to another room to get a remote-controlled Gomera face, and suddenly, the kids hear a shout from that room. They rush into the room, and Shugo tells them that his leg was stabbed by Gomera with a knife, and Gomera has run out using a different door. The kids run to find the Gomera and end up in a control room that can see the entire studio through the glass window. They see Osamu is standing around the miniature buildings and seems like he is waiting for someone. Then, the Gomera appears from behind, and Osamu asks whether it is Shugo in the Gomera outfit. Without answering his question, the Gomera attacks Osamu and kills him. Conan runs into the studio room and sees the footprints caused by the spilt paint on the floor. They follow the footprints and find Tomomi is sitting on the floor. Tomomi tells them that Gomera knocks her down and points to where Gomera has run, and the kids follow the footsteps which lead to the rooftop. At the rooftop, they don't see where the Gomera is, but Conan sees it fall to the ground with blazing fire. When Conan reaches the ground to check on the Gomera, he finds that there's nobody inside the suit.

#### File 129 - Gomera's Tragedy / The Tragedy of the Monster Gomera
Megure asks where Daisuke was when the crime occurred. Conan realises Daisuke's shirt is soaked with sweat, and Daisuke tells them that he always shuts himself in a small room without air conditioning when he works on the storyboards. Then, Megure questions Tomomi, and she points out that the person in the suit ran awkwardly, indicating the person is not accustomed to wearing the Gomera suit. Daisuke adds that it's normal since it's only Shugo who wore that suit. Megure concludes that besides Daisuke, Tomomi, and Shugo, the rest of the cast and crew were in the screening room. Agasa remembers that besides Tomomi, Ryota also left the screening room halfway when watching the unedited film. Ryota is angry with the person who put a can of paint at the set when they're going to shoot the final scene in the afternoon. One of the crew remembers that Ryota only left the screening for four to five minutes, which Conan believes is impossible for Ryota to change the suit, kill the producer, and run to the rooftop. Ryota forgets that he put the can of paint at the set until Tomomi reminds him of it. Conan asks why Shugo didn't join them at the screening room, and Ryota says nowadays, Shugo doesn't want to watch it. Daisuke says that Shugo has already prepared for Gomera to end and asked the producer to have a regular role. 

Conan is annoyed with Ayumi for continually asking for a grave for Gomera. Genta protects Ayumi and yells at Conan for being angry like Gomera. Conan wonders what it meant, and Genta tells him that the absence of tail marks on the stairs indicates Gomera was angry. Gomera always lifts his tail up when he's mad. Conan suddenly remembers something and runs to one of the rooms. After looking down to the ground from the window, he figures out the killer's trick and runs back to the studio to ask the suspects more questions.

At the studio, Shugo's injury has been treated, and they're getting ready to take him to the hospital. Shugo is too injured to play any parts in Gomera's final scene. Before Shugo leaves for the hospital, Conan asks Shugo whether there is another separate set of Gomera feet besides the claw that he showed to them earlier, and Shugo replies to him with a positive answer. Agasa asks Conan whether he figures out who the killer is. Conan has no idea yet until he sees a trace of blood on one of the knives in the box of fake knives.

#### File 130 - A Glimpse from Behind / A Silhouette Which Dissapear

Two months later, the Gomera movie is released in the cinemas. The last scene was compiled from the existing footage, and the scene of Gomera's death was cut off.

### Personal Thoughts
* The Holmes Freak Case is good, but the killer's motive seems a bit far-fetched despite the case having such a great build-up from the beginning. Anyway, with the absurdity of the world, I guess anyone can kill for any ridiculous reason.
* The killer's method in The Illustrator Murder Case is brilliant, but at the same time, it's quite complicated to understand. Actually, I'm amazed by the creativity behind the trick. I gain more understanding about the trick after watching the anime.
