### Case 031 - [Moon, Star, and Sun Case](https://www.detectiveconanworld.com/wiki/Volume_12#Moon,_Star,_and_Sun_Case)
#### Gadget Introduced - [Voice-changing Pen](https://www.detectiveconanworld.com/wiki/Voice-changing_Pen)

#### File 111 - Professor's Treasure Box / Doctor Agasa's Treasure Chest
Agasa takes the Detective Boys or Junior Detective League to his late uncle's mansion, which has been abandoned for fifty years, for a treasure hunt he had set up.

In the mansion, Conan finds a dusty old one-yen coin that was first minted about 40 years ago. Conan believes that someone had been living in the abandoned house. The coin was carved out from an original coin, but it's a smaller size than the original. Based on the character on the coin, Conan deduces that only an elderly person would use the old character. Conan also finds aluminium flakes and strands of grey hair on piles of dusty books to support his argument that there was an elderly person living in the house who liked to read books and carve coins. Meanwhile, the Detective Boys find some random objects with the symbols, which look like kids' doodles. Conan notices every line of the symbols ends with the moon, star, and sun.

Conan helps the Detective Boys to crack the clues set by Agasa. This leads them to the hidden treasure chest prepared by Agasa beforehand. However, when they open the chest, they are shocked when they see the content inside.

<br>

#### File 112 - The Black Sun
The toys inside the treasure chest are damaged, leading Conan to believe that someone was in the house between the time Agasa hid the toys and the time they got there. Agasa calls the police, but they will only come a week later to conduct an investigation. Conan suggests leaving the crime scene, but the Detective Boys are already searching for more objects with the string of symbols of a moon, star, and sun. When Ayumi finds the code on a card, Conan decides to join them to look for more. 

The group finds several objects with a string of codes, but they can't decipher the code until Conan mentions if there is a sample that they can refer to. This reminds Agasa that his aunt had received letters with the same code, and he asked Conan's father, Yusaku Kudo, for help. Yusaku deciphered the code and asked Agasa to pass the envelope to his aunt. Agasa doesn't understand why someone would send a code to his aunt, but Conan believes the code is to prevent someone from reading the letter and deduces that the sender of the letter is the same person who wrote the codes on the objects in the house. Agasa suddenly remembers that his aunt mentioned that a stranger sometimes came and peeked into her mailbox. 

Conan realises the symbols refer to the Hiragana. He matches the symbols with the Hiragana table and comes out with a chart. The Detective Boys decipher the code and find the random object based on the chart. When the code leads back to the starting point, Conan is thinking whether there is a mistake in the chart. At the same time, an unknown figure is standing outside of the window and listening to their conversations.

<br>

#### File 113 - The Nature of The Treasure / The Truth About the Treasure

Agasa suggests putting everything back to the original place before they leave the house. While the rest are busy with the placement, Conan notices that the objects from the clues are converging at the centre of the room. Then, he figures out that it refers to the chandelier. Conan twists the chandelier, and they hear something falling upstairs. They rush to the upstairs and enter one of the rooms. When Conan opens the door, they see a stairway to the attic. 

In the attic, Conan sees some partially carved one-yen coins on the floor while the kids find a skeleton of an elderly man on the desk. Conan examines the dead man and finds out that the old man died due to a bad heart while writing a letter to Agasa's aunt. Since Agasa's uncle was not a sociable person, the only visitor to the house was the painter who painted the oil painting of his uncle and aunt. There is a possibility that the dead man was the painter who knew the secret room when he came to the house. Conan also finds a printing plate for a twenty-dollar bill used for creating counterfeit money and explains that the dead man was hiding from the person who forced him to create the plates. Conan believes that the man who ordered the counterfeit money is the intruder who slashed the toys. 

Suddenly, the culprit reveals himself and mentions that he has been monitoring this mansion since he learned that the dead man is friendly with the owner. However, he never knew about the secret room until the kids cracked the clues. Conan wants to use his power-enhancing kick shoes, but the culprit shoots his gun. The culprit asks Ayumi to bring the printing plate to him, but Conan stops her and reveals the culprit's name, Tomoaki Okuda. Conan tricks him into believing ghosts are hollering around with his name and kicks the printing plate to Tomoaki. Conan ties Tomoaki with the rope. Conan later reveals that the partially written coded letter reveals the criminal's name and uses a voice-changing pen to trick the criminal. 

Two hours later, the police arrested the criminal. On the way home, the kids are sleeping at the back in the car while Agasa has a question about Conan's father. Agasa doesn't understand why Conan's father kept calling him Doctor Sun. Conan reveals it's just wordplay. He deciphers Agasa's name with the code, which every character represents by the sun symbol.

### Case 032 - [Game Convention Case](https://www.detectiveconanworld.com/wiki/Volume_12#Game_Convention_Case)
#### File 114 - An Unexpected Encounter / An Unexpected Meeting
At the Baker Hotel, Mantendo, a gaming company, is having an exhibition for their new games, including a mystery game starring Kogoro as the detective who provides hints to the players to solve the case. However, Kogoro is experiencing a hangover from the alcohol at the pre-release celebration party the night before. Kogoro mentions a few alcohol names, which reminds Conan of his main goal, finding the men in black.

At the coat check, Conan, Ran, and Kogoro encounter three of the game's developers: Hideaki Nakajima, who started the game project, Koji Ueda, who claims that the idea was from him, and Hironobu Takeshita. Koji mentions that Hideaki got his proposals approved three days after he heard Koji's idea about the game. Koji even says that Hideaki is not only good at stealing at work but also women and tries to get validation from Hironobu, but Hironobu replies that he doesn't think about it anymore since it happened back in college. Koji says that he will be generous because he heard Hideaki is having money issues and the collectors are after him. Kogoro checks in his belongings at the cloakroom. Suddenly, Hironobu points out the time, and he and Koji cut the queue to join Hideaki in checking their briefcases before the event starts. Ran notices that all three cases are identical with the company logo on them. The president, Ishikawa, appears and orders them to get to their work stations. Ran receives her cloakroom tag with the number 100, which she thinks is a sign of luck.

In the exhibition hall, Ran and Conan play various games, and Ran plays very well. Hironobu asks Hideaki to demonstrate the strength-measuring game, and Hideaki gets a high score due to a history in boxing. Hironobu asks Ran to participate in the game and tells her that it's good for releasing stress. Ran plays the game while thinking of her frustration with Shinichi, leading her in beating Hideaki's high score. Conan gives an excuse of needing to go to the bathroom, and Hideaki goes along with him. On the way, they bump into a large man dressed in black. In the bathroom, Conan gets to know that Koji, Hideaki, and Hironobu were in the same boxing club in college. Koji suddenly appears and mentions that Hideaki quit halfway and never thought that three of them would work in the same company.

After Conan leaves the bathroom and splits with Hideaki, Conan sees the large man again, using the cloakroom tag number 97 to claim a Matendo-employee briefcase. Conan finds it strange. If the large man were an employee, he should be wearing the company tie pin, but earlier when he and Hideaki bumped into him, Hideaki said that he had never seen the large man before. At first, Conan feels it's not his problem until he overhears the man introduce himself as Tequila when making a phone call. Conan hears Tequila mention a successful transaction and later will meet Gin (Melkior) and Vodka (Kaspar). Conan is so shocked that he drops his coin wallet. He sneaks a bug tracker with a transmitter on the bottom of Tequila's shoe while collecting his coins on the floor. Conan tracks Tequila into the toilets and hears him talking to himself about something not opening but something else being unlocked. A moment later, an explosion occurs in the toilets.

<br>

#### File 115 - The Whereabouts of the Bomb / Where were the Explosives?
Conan runs into the scene of the explosion and finds the shoe with his bug, confirming that Tequila is dead. As Conan tries to recall what Tequila said before the explosion, Inspector Megure arrives with an investigation team. The investigation team still couldn't identify the victim due to the state of the explosion. Megure asks if anyone saw any suspects, but none of the employees saw anything. Conan tells Megure that he saw Tequila before the victim died, adding that Hideaki bumped into the victim earlier, to Hideaki's shock. Conan describes Tequila's physical appearance and claims that Tequila was from Kansai, as he used the dialect when he was commenting on something before the blast. While the police are thinking about the suspect's motive, the president of Mantendo, Ishikawa, reveals that he received a strange letter two days ago. He thought the letter was a prank until the explosion occurred.

An investigation officer finds a key in the rubble and brings it to Megure. Ishikawa tells Megure that the key is one of the company's briefcases, and the round thing is the company's emblem. Conan wonders why Tequila had access to a briefcase and picked it up at the cloakroom. The emblem is bent backwards, which leads Conan to believe that the explosion occurred from inside the briefcase. As the police try to figure out whether Tequila was an employee or a visitor, Conan accidentally almost reveals the existence of men in black. Conan deduces that Tequila was commenting on the key that wouldn't open the case, but when it opened, the case exploded. He believes the perpetrator who gave Tequila the briefcase is also the same person with whom he had the transaction. Conan confirms with the cloakroom employee that only Tequila claimed a briefcase, which means the person who had the transaction is still in the hotel. 

Conan tells the police about the transaction partner, but they don't believe him. He wants to tell Megure, but the police stop him from interrupting the investigation. Conan protests that the murderer will escape and almost reveals his chance to find the black organisation before Ran finds him. Ran sees a cut on Conan's face. She pulls a handkerchief from her pocket, causing the claim tags with the numbers 100 and 96 to drop on the floor. Conan recalls that Tequila's claim tag number was 98. This indicates that the transaction partner was one of the three developers who intervened between Ran and Kogoro. As Conan keeps thinking about the murder, he finds that murdering Tequila at the company presentation doesn't make sense because it would be too easy for the murderer to get suspected, and it's also strange that Tequila couldn't open the briefcase.

When three of the developers reclaim their briefcases, Hideaki's number is 124, which surprises Hironobu. Koji advises the other two to check their briefcases, as they are so easy to get mixed up. Based on Conan's observation, he concludes that Tequila was an unexpected murder victim.

<br>

#### File 116 - Conan's Miscalculation / Conan's Mistake

<summary>Spoiler</summary>

Megure is releasing the employees and visitors, and Conan is worried that he will lose his lead on the Black Organisation. Conan shoots Kogoro with the tranquiliser dart, but the dart hits the cloakroom tags in Ran's back pocket. Conan attempts to retrieve that dart, causing Ran to mistake him for a pervert. She tries to kick the assumed pervert but accidentally hits Kogoro, giving Conan a chance to use him to trap the culprit.

As Kogoro, Conan calls to check Hideaki's briefcase. Hideaki tries to protect his briefcase, but the case accidentally opens when Koji tries to snatch it from Hideaki. While stacks of money fall from the case, Hironobu screams and ducks for cover, revealing he was the one who planted the bomb in the briefcase. Hideaki was the transaction partner and stealthily swapped the claim tags along with the keys with Tequila when he bumped into him. Tequila used the claim tag to pick up Hideaki's briefcase and went to the bathroom to confirm its contents. Tequila was having trouble with the key and the lock because Hironobu had swapped the briefcase with another. Hironobu had swapped the tags when Hideaki took off his jacket. To avoid suspicions, Hironobu cut in between Kogoro and Ran so his claim number would be in the nineties, similar to Hideaki. The briefcase that Hironobu is holding was the one supposed to hand to the victim. The blown-up briefcase was Hironobu's.

Hironobu claims that he never thought the explosion came from the bomb he planted. He had sent a threatening letter to make it look like an act of terrorism. When the police are dragging Hironobu away, he yells at Hideaki for killing Yoshimi. Koji reveals that Yoshimi was their college's boxing club's manager. She committed suicide after Hideaki broke up with her. She was Hironobu's girlfriend since junior high before she became Hideaki's girlfriend.

Conan urges Hideaki to explain all he knows about Tequila and the organisation, but all Hideaki knows is that he met Tequila at a "Cocktail" bar located in the Daikoku building. Megure tries to make Kogoro explain more about the organisation while Conan rushes to the Daikoku building. However, by the time Conan is in the lift to the top floor, a massive explosion suddenly happens. The police arrive after one hour. The horrific explosion has completely destroyed the bar, and the police couldn't even determine whether anyone was inside. Conan later learns that Hideaki received a big sum of money from the men in black in exchange for a list of the world's top programmers. Conan does not know whether Gin and Vodka are still alive, and the case remains an unsolved mystery.

### Case 033 - Holmes Freak Case / Holmes Enthusiasts Murder Case
#### File 117 - Gathering at Mycroft / The Meeting at Mycroft
Ran and Kogoro tag along with Conan to participate in the Sherlock Holmes Freak Tour. The inn employee, Hitomi Iwai, drives them and other participants to the inn. The other participants are as follows:
* Ikuo Kawatsu – High school gym teacher.
* Toshiaki Fujisawa - Train station employee.
* Nanako Shimizu – Librarian.

The group will be staying at the Mycroft Inn. While Ran and Conan are looking for their room, Conan bumps into another participant, Maria Toda, a fortune teller. Maria claims that Conan is hiding a terrible secret and asks him whether he's hiding his bad grade on a test. Conan wonders whether she knows his secret but quickly dismisses it.

Hiroyuki Kanaya introduces himself as the owner of the inn and the organiser of the tour. When Hiroyuki mentions the extensive screening of the participants, Toshiaki doubts the screening process after learning that Kogoro has no knowledge of Sherlock Holmes during their journey to the inn. Kogoro wants to leave, but Hiroyuki tells everyone that he invites Kogoro to join the tour and claims that Kogoro is comparable to Sherlock Holmes. Then, Kento Togano, the president of the mystery club at his university, and Ayako Oki recognise Kogoro but dismiss the claim that Kogoro is comparable to Holmes. Conan tries to calm Kogoro, and Kogoro gives Conan a scold, revealing Conan's name. The participants are excited with Conan's name and like him more. Ran feels relieved to see Conan behaves like a child and reminds her of Shinichi, who is a big fan of Sherlock Holmes. Heiji Hattori suddenly appears, and Ran asks him whether he is also a Holmes fan. Heiji clarifies that he applied for the tour, hoping to catch up with Shinichi, and claims that he's more into Ellery Queen but changes his statement after the other participants glare at him. Hiroyuki informs everyone about the schedule tomorrow before letting the participants retire to their room. There will be a quiz tomorrow, and the winner will get the first edition of Conan Doyle's A Study in Scarlet. The maid is giving them the test paper which has 1000 questions, and only those who score 990 or above will earn the right to participate in the quiz after tomorrow's dinner. Those who score lower will be asked to leave the inn. The cellphones and any books related to Holmes have to hand them over to the maid. 

The next day, everyone has meals together and waits for the owner, Hiroyuki, to appear. They ask the maid to get Hiroyuki to grade their test, but the maid tells them that she was asked to attend to the guests until after dinner. Meanwhile, Heiji is bored without Shinichi's appearance. The group waited for Hiroyuki until after midnight, but he still did not appear. The participants are angry and return to the room. Kogoro plans to go back to bed while Ran, Conan, and Heiji continue staying in the living room. Ayako asks Kento to leave together, but Kento claims that whoever leaves will be a loser. Suddenly, they see a car driving past, and the driver appears to be the owner, but the car is heading to the cliff. Heiji and Conan rush out and try to stop the driver, but the car is speeding up. In the end, the car falls off from the cliff.

<br>

#### File 118 - The Women who Knew Too Much
Heiji confirms that the owner dressed like Holmes, and he plunged into the ocean. Kogoro deduces that Hiroyuki doesn't want to let go of his first edition book, but Kento disagrees and claims that Hiroyuki was murdered. Kento suspects someone drugged or killed the owner beforehand and then put the car in gear. Heiji disagrees because the car sped up in the end, and Kento defends his theory in an unconfident way. Heiji mentions that there was a strange sound in the car, and the sound resembles the sound of wind, but others suspect it might have been the air conditioner. Kogoro suggests waiting for the police in the inn. Conan is looking for something on the ground and bumps his head with Heiji's again. Heiji says that he's checking the ground for any indications that the air conditioner was turned on. Heiji begins to suspect Conan because Conan bumped his head with him in the previous case when they were looking for clues.

When the guests return to the inn, they find out that all the phones have been broken and the first edition is gone. Toshiaki wants to leave, but Heiji says that someone punctured the fuel tank in the other car, and the fuel has already drained out. Heiji believes it's a murder, and the murderer traps them in the inn so he can continue with his killing. The guests accuse each other while Kogoro asks the maid about the security cameras. The maid reveals the security cameras are props to prevent the guests from cheating. Heiji claims that those who stayed in the living room for several hours before the crime are innocent. Toshiaki asks whether he and those who left the room are the suspects. Ayako asks Heiji whether he found anything earlier, and Heiji replies that he didn't find anything. Ayako suddenly laughs and claims that she has the evidence and knows who the murderer is. 

Ayako says that she's giving a ten-minute grace period for the murderer to confess, or else she will denounce the murderer herself. She leaves for the bathroom, and Kento goes with her for protection. Heiji and Conan believe Ayako has something that they don't know about, leading Ran to recall that Ayako was acting strange in the afternoon. Ran saw Ayako after lunch, and Ayako quickly hid something behind her back when she saw Ran. The guests begin accusing Ayako, probably of sneaking into other rooms to cheat on the test. When Kento returns, they question him about the killer, but Kento says Ayako didn't tell him and locked herself in the bathroom. The maid reveals that besides Ayako, Nanako and Kento skipped lunch. This leads Conan to ask Kento whether Ayako went to his room during lunch. Kento claims that he was alone in the room to finish the test during lunchtime, leading Heiji and Conan to believe that Ayako saw something in someone else's room and the suspect is one of the four people without an alibi. 

Ran wonders why Ayako still hasn't returned from the bathroom. The other guests feel worried and rush to the bathroom. Ayako criticises their rude behaviour, and when Toshiaki asks her about the murderer, she changes her mind and thinks maybe she made a mistake. Heiji and Conan feel there are many questions unanswered in the case. When they return to the living room, they find out that Ayako went for a walk and said that there will be no more murders. Since all the possible suspects are in the room, Kento believes there should be no more murders. Ikuo suggests one of them walk to the train station and call the police. Suddenly, everyone hears an explosion from outside, and all of them rush out. They see a big blast of fire at the garage.

<br>

#### File 119 - The Mysterious Explosion
When the guests finally put out the fire, they go into the garage, and Kento sees Ayako's body in the back seat of the car. Ikuo suspects whether the owner really died or it's a fake suicide, but Heiji and Conan convince him that they saw the driver was the owner. Kogoro suggests staying together and waiting in the inn.

The maid plans to prepare some snacks and coffee. Ran offers to help the maid. Ikuo and Kento tag along to protect them. Kogoro and Maria are discussing the case in the living room. Kogoro mentions the mystery quiz, and Toshiaki gets angry about receiving a card, asking him to go to the garage at 5 am to get the first edition book. Conan asks Toshiaki where he got the card, and Toshiaki replies that he got it when he went back to his room after dinner the night before. Kogoro comes out with the assumption that the owner is still alive, which angers Heiji. Heiji criticises Kogoro. Coincidentally, Ran comes in with the food, and she defends her father's detective skills after she hears Heiji's criticism. Heiji feels suspicious and drags Ran away for a private conversation. Heiji asks Ran where Conan is every time Kogoro makes his deductions. After hearing Ran's response, Heiji asks Conan whether he helps Kogoro by hiding behind. Conan answers that he loves to play detective. Suddenly, a blackout occurs.  Toshiaki lights up his lighter and offers it to the maid who wants to go to the circuit breaker. Suddenly, they hear a stabbing sound. When the lights come back, they find Toshiaki is injured and an icepick is on his arm. Kogoro suspects the attacker escaped through the window after seeing the broken glasses. However, Heiji claims that the suspect is still in the room, and Conan finally understands how the suspect set the garage on fire without leaving the room.

<br>

#### File 120 - The Lie is Revealed
Kogoro claims that the owner is still alive and accuses him of attacking Toshiaki. Toshiaki finds it hard to believe and reveals that the owner self-published a book called 'Irene Adler Scoffs' the year before, and he contributed an essay to the book in exchange for admission to that tour. Kogoro finds it strange that Toshiaki is the only participant who was acquainted with the owner until the maid reveals that Ayako's father is an old friend of the owner, which gives Ayako the chance to participate in the tour every year. Heiji notices that one of the chairs is in a strange position. Conan finds a shard of glass stuck in the chair. Heiji deduces that the suspect used the chair to break the window and shoved it back to the table before the lights came back.

The maid tells everyone the fuse blew after she tried to plug in the coffee maker. She then leads them to the kitchen, and Heiji finds a thin wire wrapped around the prongs which caused the fuse to blow. Conan finds another gift's tag with a wire which indicates the culprit used it to tie on the prongs. Kogoro accuses Ikuo, but Heiji still feels something is not right and asks Conan for validation. Conan continues with his deduction, which surprises everyone, and he quickly changes his tone and acts as a kid who doesn't know anything. Ran takes him away while Conan finds it difficult to work on the case when Heiji is around. Heiji begins to suspect Conan's real identity and recalls Conan was missing every time Shinichi appeared, but he dismisses the ridiculous idea in the end.

Heiji analyses the first incident and believes the owner has already died when he was driving the car. The rigor mortis had caused the owner's grip on the steering wheel to become firm. Based on Heiji's analysis, he concludes that the owner was killed before dinner the day before. However, Heiji doesn't understand why the killer needs to put the owner in the car so early. Kento believes the murderer places the owner in the car in advance in order to stiffen the owner's position. The other guests question how the car could suddenly speed up since the dead man no longer can control the car. Suddenly, Heiji figures out who the killer is, and so do Conan and Kogoro. Kogoro accuses Maria of being the killer by hypnotising the owner into driving the car. Conan wishes to drug Kogoro to use him as a mouthpiece but is worried that Heiji might find out. He asks Heiji what happens to the rigor mortis when the outside temperature rises, but Heiji pretends that he doesn't know the answer. Conan knows Heiji is trying to trap him, and he gives an excuse of going to the bathroom. Heiji wants to follow him but can't find Conan after opening the door. Conan hides on top of the door and shoots a tranquiliser dart on Heiji. Conan hides behind Heiji to reveal the killer.
