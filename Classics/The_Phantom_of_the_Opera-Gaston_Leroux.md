# The Phantom of the Opera - Gaston Leroux

## Summary
On the retirement day of the opera's managers, a group of dancers are discussing the existence of a ghost in the opera. They are terrified despite not being sure whether the ghost really appears in the opera. Joseph Buquet, who is the chief scene-shifter, was telling everyone that he has seen the ghost and gave a full description of it. While the dancers are talking about the ghost that they have never seen before, the mother of one of the dancers comes to the room and tells them that Joseph has died by hanging himself on the third-cellar floor. Everyone in the room suspects that it was an act from the ghost.

Count Philippe de Chagny's younger brother, Raoul, wishes to have a private conversation with Christine behind the stage after her performance. Christine is resting in her room after recovering from the faint. She wishes to be left alone. However, Raoul, who is waiting outside of the room, overhears Christine having an intimate conversation with a man. After Christine leaves the room, he goes inside to look for the man who Christine was having a conversation with, but to no avail.

The previous managers have warned their successors, Moncharmin and Richard, about the opera ghost. However, the two new managers treat it as a joke despite receiving a personal letter from the opera ghost. They ignore the instructions to please the opera ghost and sell the seats in Box Five which are specifically reserved for him. Later, a strange incident occurs in Box Five, causing disruptions to the viewers. The new managers question the inspector and the box-keeper, who believe that it was the opera ghost who was disturbing the patrons in Box Five.

After the massive success at the gala, Christine is afraid to sing again and isolates herself. On the anniversary of her father's death, she visits the town of Perros-Guirec. She sends a letter to Raoul, telling him about her plan as she remembers their childhood together. Raoul takes the trip to Perros and later confesses his love to her. She rejects his advance, which frustrates Raoul, leading him to confront her about the mysterious man in the room on that day. Later, she reveals that the mysterious man is the "Angel of Music" and reminds Raoul that her late father used to tell her about the existence of such a character and promised to bring the angel to her when he goes to heaven. Raoul finds it ridiculous, which angers Christine. On that night, Christine leaves her room and goes to the church. Raoul follows her behind without her knowledge. He hears a beautiful music which used to be played by old Mr Daaé. However, strange things happen after the music stops, and he is found lying half-dead at the church altar the next day. The innkeeper informs Christine, and she successfully revives Raoul.

On the Saturday morning, the managers receive a letter from the opera ghost. The letters include the request for the reservation of the private box, Box 5, and the demand for Christine to sing the special part on that evening, or else the managers will face the consequences. Meanwhile, the diva, Carlotta, also received a threat, asking her not to sing that night. Carlotta has been jealous of Christine's success after replacing her at the gala and chooses to ignore the threat. On that evening, the managers watch the performance at Box Five. At the beginning, the performance goes smoothly until Carlotta's turn to sing her verse. When Carlotta tries to repeat the verse, she experiences a croaked voice while the managers watch the horror scene from Box 5. The performance is further interrupted when a chandelier falls in the opera, which causes a few injuries and a death.

Christine invites Raoul to a masked ball. They argue again, which leads Christine to end their relationship or friendship. Christine goes to her dressing room without knowing that Raoul is hiding inside. She talks to herself and mentions the name "Erik". The opera ghost sings a beautiful song, and Raoul experiences an illusion while listening to the angelic voice. After the illusion ends, Christine is no longer in the room, leaving Raoul sobbing and asking himself who Erik is.

Raoul visits Christine the next day and sees that she wears a wedding ring, and he confronts her about the name Erik. Christine asks Raoul to forget about Erik and never enter her dressing room except if she asks for him. The following day, at the opera, Raoul tells her that he will depart in a month for the North Pole expedition. Christine is happy that she will never see him again; she probably worries about his safety when the opera ghost is around. She knows that they can't get married but suggests having a secret engagement for a month so she can enjoy the temporary love without offending the opera ghost.

Raoul suggests they elope, but Christine wishes to sing the last time for Erik the next day. She asks Raoul to fetch her in her dressing room at midnight while Erik is waiting for her in the dining room at a house near the lake. Christine explains to Raoul that she heard the voice of the opera ghost three months ago. Her adopted mother misled her into believing that it was the "Angel of Music". The opera ghost admitted that he was the angel and gave her lessons every day for the past three months. Christine also reveals the phantom's lair after he kidnaps her in trying to win her heart. She reveals that Erik is a human being and not a ghost. During her time with him in the lair, she unmasked the phantom and was shocked by his horrific deformed face. Christine has no hate for him but only fears. She loves Raoul but is reluctant to leave Erik, as she has mixed feelings of fear and sympathy.

The next day, Philippe confronts Raoul regarding the news of Raoul's marriage with Christine. He objects to their relationship, but Raoul determines to flee with Christine on that night and prepares for the journey. In the evening, a blackout occurs unexpectedly during the performance, and Christine disappears when the lights return.

The police tell Raoul that Philippe has taken Christine, and he rushes to chase after him but stops by the Persian. The Persian offers to help Raoul, and together they head to Erik's secret lair with guns brought by the Persian servant. At the torture chamber, they hear a conversation between Erik and Christine in the next room. When Erik temporarily leaves the room to handle an intruder, they find out from Christine that Erik forces her to make a choice before 11 next evening. She has to either marry him or he will kill them and everyone in the Opera House by using explosives.

## Ending
<details>
<summary>Spoiler</summary>

- The Persian and Raoul almost drown to death in the torture chamber. They are saved last minute after Christine begs Erik to rescue them, and she agrees to marry him. When Erik is alone with Christine, he lifts his mask to kiss her on her forehead and eventually receives a kiss in return. Erik reveals he has never kissed anyone, including his mother, who would run away if he ever tried to kiss her. Moved, he and Christine cried together. She also holds his hand and says, "Poor, unhappy Erik", which reduces him to "a dog ready to die for her". He released Christine and Raoul, and they have eloped together for a quiet life. 
- After the incident in the torture chamber, the Persian later awakes in his room at home. Erik later pays him a visit and tells him everything. Erik dies of a broken heart a few weeks later. Before he releases Christine, he has made her promise to come back to bury him with the golden ring once she sees the news of his death.

## Personal Thoughts
- Safe to say, it's not everyone's cup of tea.
- I feel the story itself is interesting, and I can accept the characters' absurdity, but I couldn't bear the torture chamber scene. It's too long, and the overwhelming details of the tricks didn't help with my limited imagination. However, Erik's last interaction with Christine was really touching, and what he had done for her with her lover was indeed a heroic act.
