# Detectice Conan Volume 10 - East Meets West

## Summary
### Case 024 - Wealthy Daughter Murder Case
#### File 091 - The Water Time-Difference Trick

<details>
  <summary>Spoiler</summary>

Conan reveals the murderer is Takashi. When they were looking for Reika the first time, he lured Yuji by telling him that he saw Reika near the fountain and attacked him from behind.

To turn off the lights automatically for the blackout, Takashi set the timer on the remote of all the heaters to turn on all the heaters at the same time. He also turned on the microwaves simultaneously, causing the spike in electricity usage, and the breaker tripped. Then, he broke the window in the darkness to make it appear as if the murderer cut the lights off and fled. 

When Takashi spiked the coffee, he didn't care who took the drink as long as he was not the one who drank it. Takashi didn't kill Ran because he wanted to get Ran's testimony that the murderer forced her head into the water. The testimony is to prove that Reika's murder is similar to the first two incidents. In fact, Reika drowned to death after the murderer filled the bathtub using the shower and secured her to the tub with the duct tape. Takashi adjusted the flow of water so she would be drowned to death between 5 and 6 am. Then, during that time, he stayed at the parlour with the others to create an alibi. Around 7 in the morning, he pretended to use the bathroom, but he actually went to pull the lifeless Reika out of the tub. To avoid anyone from finding Reika taped to the tub, he broke the light bulbs in the area and even acted to check the tub himself as if he were looking for Reika. Conan points out the water blotches on Takashi's elbow, indicating he got wet when pulling Reika out of the tub. 

Conan makes a guess that Reika's murder is probably related to Yaeko. Takashi suddenly shouts that Yaeko's death was not an accident but caused by Yuji and Reika. He begins telling the story about the accident two years ago when Yaeko went to the island on that stormy day and found Yuji and Reika were there. She was wearing a life jacket and carried an extra for Reika. Yuji and Reika were saved because they took Yaeko's life jacket. Takashi tells them that Yuji and Reika told him the truth before he killed them. Takashi feels both of them deserved to die, but Yone rebukes him. She admits that she couldn't completely forgive Reika, but she doesn't believe anyone should go through the same thing as her, grieving the loss of someone.

The next day, Ran tells Sonoko everything that happened in the villa. Ran tells Sonoko that she couldn't help thinking Shinichi was there when she was attacked but later only knew it was Conan who saved her. Ran teases that Shinichi is just a mystery geek, and Sonoko agrees that Shinichi would never be so attentive and kind.

<details>
<summary>Manga vs Anime - Reference from the Site</summary>

  * In the manga version, Reika announces to her father and the guests that she intends to marry one of the suitors at the party. This scene is removed in the anime.
  * Reika wears a white coat over her dress when she is about to leave the house with her father after the party has ended in the anime.
  * Some of Reika's expressions and mannerisms were changed to be less malevolent, leaning more towards being childish, compared to the manga.
  * In the anime, after Yone puts a blanket on Ran, who is sleeping on a sofa, she looks at Ran's face and is reminded of her granddaughter Yaeko.
  * In the anime, Ichieda doesn't drink the coffee served by Ran and Yone, as he has previously drugged one of the cups.
  * In the anime, Conan states through Kogoro that Takashi simply avoided the drugged coffee by not drinking it, whereas in the manga, he speculates that Takashi must have marked the cup with a spoon or placed the cup in a recognisable position.
  * In the anime, after Yone is angered by Takashi's cruel remarks and saddened by Reika's passing, she also closes the lifeless Reika's eyes in sign of paying respect to her.
