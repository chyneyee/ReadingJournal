# A Song of Isolation - Michael J. Malone

## Summary
Amelie Hart is a popular actress despite only appearing in four movies. But at the peak of her career, she leaves the fame for her boyfriend, Dave, who has an ordinary job. Everyone is shocked and wonders what leads her to make such a bold move. As she hopes the publicity will die down after four years, the media turns into a frenzy when her boyfriend is arrested on charges of child sexual abuse. Dave insists that he's innocent, and Amelie is supportive of him. However, the media escalates to daily harassment, forcing Amelie to flee the country.

While Dave is locked up in the jail and Amelie relocates for a quiet life, the victim, Damaris, is trying to understand what happens to the adult world.

## Personal Thoughts
* From the beginning of the story, I didn't really like the writing style because I found it difficult to follow the story. I chose to stick until the end because I wanted to know whether Dave was guilty and what happened to Amelie a few years before. 
* The unorganised storyline and the random irrelevant details make it even harder to keep up with the story's progress. The writing is so rushed, and after providing a brief update of a character, the story quickly pushes the reader to move on to the next stage.
* The best things from the story are the court scene and Damaris's confrontation with her parents. I'm speechless with the ending, as the focus is on the happy ending for the main characters, and the rest are just a quick update.
