# Convenience Store Woman - Sayaka Murata, Ginny Tapley Takemori (Translator)

## Summary
- The narrator, Keiko Furukura, describes her routine daily job at a convenience store.
- Keiko has been working in the same convenience store since she was eighteen years old. She is 36 now.
- She feels comfortable working as a convenience store worker, which she compares to a cog in society. The job makes her feel like a normal person.
- The sale of food varies according to the weather.
- Most workers are university students or job-hoppers.
- Her friends don't understand why she stays in a casual job for so long and is still single.
- Since Keiko was a child, she has always acted spontaneously without thinking further to resolve a problem, but the solution always led others to feel that she is an odd person. As time goes by, she feels worried and anxious that she might act wrongly and be judged by others; she chooses to observe and follow others for safety measures.
- Now as an adult, she receives tremendous pressure from peers and society for being single and working in a convenience store. She needs guidance and always follows advice from others so she can be a normal person.

<br>

## Personal Thoughts
- It resembles a diary written by a convenience store worker who is likely autistic and excels in her routine tasks but struggles to understand human behaviours.
- This story is depressing to read. From being questioned for her unmarried status to working in a convenience store, she then has to find a man to quiet others or be "normal" to survive in society.
- The bluntness of Shiraha exposes the reality of life, and there are still many people who think like him. He thinks he is smarter and better than others, but actually, his personality is lower than anyone's. 
- Is it truly acceptable to have a man in life regardless of whether he's a disdainful person? I'm not questioning the narrator's action but rather the opinions from the people around her.
- Finally, she made a decision for herself to be her true self. For once, she follows her heart without the need to observe or seek advice from others.
